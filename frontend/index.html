About this project

NazarVyapar was built by Siddharth Kumar to give Indian small business owners — kirana stores, salons, medical shops, clothing outlets — the same footfall intelligence that large retail chains pay lakhs for, at a total hardware cost of under ₹300. No subscription. No vendor lock-in. No data sold to advertisers.

By using NazarVyapar you acknowledge that you are accessing a tool designed in good faith to solve a real problem for real people.

Real-world impact

Problem How NazarVyapar solves it
"I don't know my peak hours" Hourly footfall bar chart updated in real time
"My offer didn't work?" Before / after footfall comparison by day
"Hire extra help on Sunday?" Weekly pattern view across 7 days
"Does lighting affect customers?" LDR vs footfall correlation chart

Data collected

The system collects entry count events from your PIR sensor and ambient light readings (LDR analog values). No personal data — no faces, no biometrics, no identifiers — is ever collected, processed, or transmitted. All data is stored in your own MongoDB Atlas instance and never shared with third parties.

Your data, your server

NazarVyapar is entirely self-hosted. Your ESP32 posts data directly to your own Express backend. Siddharth Kumar and all project contributors have zero access to your store's data at any time.

Telegram alerts

If you enable the daily summary bot, your Telegram chat ID and bot token are stored only in your own .env file on your server. They are never transmitted to any NazarVyapar service.

ML peak-hour predictions

Predictions run entirely on your local machine using scikit-learn and a locally saved model.pkl. No data leaves your network during inference.

Limitation of liability

NazarVyapar is provided as-is, without warranty of any kind. While every effort has been made to ensure accuracy of footfall counts, PIR sensors (HC-SR501) may occasionally misfire in high-heat or high-reflectivity environments. Business decisions based on this data are made entirely at your own discretion and risk.

Developer & creator — NazarVyapar
Siddharth built NazarVyapar after seeing firsthand how small shop owners make critical business decisions — staffing, timings, promotions — without any data to back them up. Enterprise footfall systems cost ₹50,000+, putting them far beyond what a kirana owner earning ₹30,000/month can afford. His answer: a full stack spanning ESP32 firmware, an Express + MongoDB backend, a Chart.js dashboard, a Telegram summary bot, and a scikit-learn peak-hour predictor — all reproducible for under ₹300 in hardware. The goal is simple: bring retail intelligence to every small business in India.
